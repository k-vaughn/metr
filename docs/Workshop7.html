METR - Workshop 7

Campus Governance

Questions for METR Workshop 7: Campus Governance

The seventh METR workshop will include focus on the following questions:

  • What are the minimum requirements to become an authorized jurisdictional entity that can issue rules?
  • Are there any restrictions on the size of a jurisdictional entity to participate in METR?
  • What is the process for a small campus or town to become authorized to submit rules?
  • Should a METR jurisdictional entity be allowed to use a proxy (e.g., a small entity asking a larger or specialty entity to manage its rules)?
  • How should campus regulators interact with METR as compared to governmental regulators?
  • How do user systems know the territorial boundaries for each jurisdiction?
  • What is the process for defining and approving the electronic representation of a jurisdictional boundary (including campuses)?
  • What is the METR process associated with handling a jurisdiction being created or being subsumed into another entity?
  • What should happen to existing rules when the jurisdictional entity for a campus or facility changes?
  • To what extent does METR need to indicate that a small campus facility (e.g., a shopâ€™s parking area) is not covered by any additional rules (e.g., as opposed to not supporting METR)?
  • What implications on METR components might there be in dealing with a transport facility that straddles or is near a jurisdictional boundary?
  • How quickly do jurisdictional changes need to be implemented on transport user systems?
